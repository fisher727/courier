Security

Security Information.

We regret that attempts are made again and again by third parties to misuse the DPD brand for fraudulent purposes.

Here you will learn everything which is important about current risks relating to the subject of the Internet and emails, and how you can protect yourself against these risks. Please note that fraud is only successful as long as it is not detected and recognised for what it is.

Please note also that DPD accepts no liability for any damage which is caused to you as a result of such fraudulent activity!

If you have any questions or wish to provide us with information you can also contact us direct at any time.

Security instructions

To our great regret the DPD brand is currently being used for attempted fraud. This takes the form of favourably priced apartments on online real estate portals. DPD is shown there as an agency for these apartments, offering to send interested parties a key to view the apartments in return for a payment (claimed to be to DPD).

IMPORTANT: we wish to point out that DPD does not carry out any business activities in the real estate field. DPD does not implement services of this kind for real estate portals or estate agents, and does not implement any financial transactions for this purpose. Any demands for payment in this connection are attempts at fraud and are made with the unauthorised use of the DPD name and brand.

DPD urgently warns you not to respond to such demands and not to make payments of this kind! DPD accepts no liability for any damage or loss which results from such payments. In such cases we advise you to report the matter to the police without delay.

If you are confronted by such demands or similar requests and are not sure if the relevant communications really come from DPD and are genuine, you can contact us at any time at [email protected].

We apologise for any inconvenience!

We regret that at present there are emails in circulation which fraudulently give the impression that they come from DPD. These are fake messages to consignees for the DPD services Predict and New Delivery. In the fake message the email recipient is requested to open an attachment. This attachment contains a virus.

IMPORTANT: Do not open an email attachment of this kind! The email should be deleted as soon as it has been identified as fake.

DPD accepts no liability for any damage which is caused to your computer (including data loss and its consequences) by such a virus. In such cases we advise you to report the matter to the police without delay.

This is how to distinguish the fake emails from real messages sent by DPD:

Consignee emails from DPD have the following features:

  • The sender of the email is always: [email protected]
  • The shipment number is always linked to the DPD tracking function. If no parcel can be found in the parcel history, it must be assumed that the parcel label number is false.
  • The original email always contains the information about who the consignor of the parcel is. For example: "Your shipment 12345678901234 from CONSIGNOR NAME will probably be delivered on…."
  • An alternative delivery date can only be arranged by email via the link to: "http://wwww.paketnavigator.at".
  • DPD does not send any attachments in its consignee emails!

If you are confronted by such demands or similar requests and are not sure if the relevant communications really come from DPD and are genuine, you can contact us at any time at [email protected].

We apologise for any inconvenience you may have been caused!
